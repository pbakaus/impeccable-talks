## ACT II · The wrong altitude

**07 · The roles are blurring** *(designer / engineer → design engineer)*
- "For thirty years there were two people: a designer who decided, an engineer who built.
  Those lines are blurring: the designer codes, the coder designs. Design isn't handed off
  anymore; it lives in the running product. That's who I'm here for."

**08 · The spectrum** *(pixels · ? · prompts)*
- "Every design tool sits somewhere on this line. Two ends. Both are what we ship today. Both
  wrong for a world where the designer *is* the engineer. The answer's the middle, and
  nobody built a tool there."

**09 · Center a div** *(ItakGol video, auto-plays)*
- 🎙️ Let the video run a beat. "Here's the state of the art. This is Itamar using Claude
  Opus, a frontier model, to center a div." Gesture at the blowtorch. "The most powerful
  tools ever built, pointed at the tiniest task. Wrong altitude, live."

**10 · Too low. Pixels.** *(the inspector panel)*
- "So, the low end. Padding sliders, hex fields, tracking: one property at a time, by hand,
  silent about *why*. The altitude of handing pixels to an engineer…"
- 🎙️ "…except there's no engineer left to hand them to. It's just you, 11pm, nudging a
  border-radius by one pixel, alone."

**11 · 2022 slop** *(full-bleed, no text)*
- "When I said slop a minute ago, this is what you pictured. Purple gradients, glass, six
  emoji. You called it on sight."

**12 · 2026 slop** *(the reveal)*
- "Here's the uncomfortable part." Beat. "*This* is also slop. It just put on a nice sweater:
  cream, a tasteful serif, one accent. Six other startups have this exact page this week.
  Slop is a moving target; you'll never out-run it with a better prompt."

**13 · You can't one-shot design** *(phrase)*
- "Which brings me to the fantasy a shelf of tools is selling: type a sentence, the problem
  of design goes away. One shot. It doesn't work, and on the day it *looks* like it did, you
  own none of it and your real problem's still there. You can generate a page in one shot.
  You cannot *design* in one shot."

**14 · Context-rich, multi-shot** *(~~one-shot~~)*
- "Real design is the opposite of one-shot. It's context-rich, and it's multi-shot: a brief,
  then you *iterate*. Both halves. Skip either and you're back to slop-in-a-sweater."

## ACT III · The brief

**15 · "Make it pop"** *(meme pile, YOU build it, one click each)*
- "And the direction we hand these tools is usually garbage. What does the AI, or the
  stakeholder, actually get?"
- 🎙️ *(click)* "'If you could just make it pop… that'd be great.'" *(click)* "'Tell me to
  make it pop again, I dare you.'" *(click)* "One does not simply *make the design pop.*"
  Let each land. Then turn it: "That's not direction. It's a shrug."

**16 · The three questions** *(brief)*
- "A real brief is where intent gets decided, mostly three questions: what's the emotional
  territory, what should this *never* feel like, what's the reference."
- 🎙️ "No AI design tool asks you a single one of these. It just… starts. Like a designer who
  moves your logo before you've said hello."

**17 · Nobody decided anything here** *(dissected slop, dimmed)*
- "Skip the brief and you still get *output*. You get this: technically fine, and nobody
  decided anything. Mass-produce taste and you get *algorithmic Uniqlo*: pleasant, completely
  interchangeable."

## ACT IV · The answer: adjectives

**18 · The spectrum fills: adjectives** *(the answer)*
- "So back to the line. The altitude in the middle, the one nobody built for, is the one a
  director already works at. Adjectives."

**19 · The word wall** *(the vocabulary)*
- "Warmer. Leaner. Bolder. Distill. Add tension. The controls a director thinks in. Not a
  parameter in sight."
- 🎙️ "Yes, half of these are verbs: distill, polish. 'Adjective' is my shorthand for the
  altitude, not a grammar lecture. If a linguist's in the room, I'm sorry, and please stay
  for the black hole."

**20 · Same brief, three adjectives** *(Dusk cards, the peak)*
- "Proof it's a control, not a vibe. Same brief: a fragrance called Dusk. One default, what
  the model ships unasked. Then three decisions: bolder, quieter, distill. Same brief, three
  points of view. *That's* an adjective doing work."

**21 · Bolder, how?** *(the objection + the reflex)*
- "The objection I'd raise if I were you: isn't an adjective just a short prompt? Watch what a
  raw model does with *make it bolder*." Gesture at the reflex render. "It reaches for the
  gradient, the glass, the neon, the *opposite* of bold." *(Callback: "same thing you watched
  happen in the first sixty seconds.")* "The word's wide open, so it fills the gap with its
  own defaults."

**22 · The definition**
- "What closes that gap is a *definition* both sides share. `/bolder` throws out the reflexes
  first, no gradients, no glass, no neon, then says what bold actually is: hierarchy, scale,
  decisive type. And yes, that definition is a prompt, you just shouldn't be *writing* it
  live. It's one a community argued over and hardened. The real axis was never pixels versus
  prompts. It's ad-hoc versus curated."

**23 · The test**
- "My favorite test, from the skill itself: show someone your work and say 'AI made this
  bolder.' If they believe you, you failed."

**24 · An adjective is a leitwort** *(coinage + credit)*
- "There's a name for a word like this. A *leitwort*: a leading word, from literary theory.
  Matt Pocock noticed it in AI skills: a word the agent repeats to steer its own behavior.
  That's exactly what a good adjective is. Give the model a leitwort, and it leads itself."
  *(Credit Matt out loud, he's one of us.)*

## ACT V · The honest caveat + what I learned

**25 · The descent** *(altitude shifts, this is the workflow)*
- "One honest caveat before you hold me to 'prompts are always wrong.' The right altitude
  *moves*. At the very start, deciding what the thing even is, a prompt is exactly right. At
  the very end, you want your hands on the pixels. Impeccable owns the productive middle."
- **The workflow point:** "And notice what that line is: a *workflow*: explore, iterate,
  polish, ship. Impeccable's commands map to it on purpose."

**26 · Map the workflow first** *(the builder's lesson)*
- "This is the lesson I'd tattoo on every tool-builder here, skill authors included. You can
  build a beautiful tool, a perfect golden puzzle piece, and get zero adoption, because it
  doesn't fit the puzzle. The puzzle is the workflow."
- 🎙️ "I've built the gorgeous puzzle piece that fits nothing. More than once. Expensive way
  to learn nobody asked." → "Map the workflow first."

**27 · /overdrive** *(the event-horizon shader, plays once)*
- Let it come up; let them look. "This is `/overdrive`: the command that pushes an interface
  past reasonable. Shaders, spring physics, 60fps."
- 🎙️ "I almost cut it. Thought it was too much, a little embarrassing. Ran it on radiant
  expecting to hate it, and it built *this*." (gesture at the black hole) "Rabid fan base now.
  I ship what people pull, not what I'd defend on a slide."

**28 · An adjective with nothing behind it is just a nicer prompt** *(the lesson)*
- "What *didn't* work: an adjective with nothing behind it. 'Warmer' with no brief just
  regresses to the average. The vocabulary rides on top of intent. It doesn't replace it."

## ACT VI · The one it will never ship + close

**29 · /auto** *(tweets stamp in → the meme slams over, auto-plays)*

- 🎙️ When Michael Scott lands, just gesture and wait for the laugh. "This is the single

**30 · There is no /auto** *(the answer)*
- "There is no `/auto`, and there never will be. It's the one-shot fantasy in a nicer coat.
  Hand design to `/auto` and you're back to output without intent, you own none of it.
  Keeping a human in the loop isn't friction. It's the entire reason this works."

**31 · Taste can be amplified. It can't be lab-grown.**
- "Here's what a year taught me: you cannot teach a model taste. We tried. It doesn't move.
  What you *can* do is hand it sharper instruments and leave the seeing to the person holding
  them. Taste can be amplified. It can't be lab-grown."

## Delivery notes
- **Cold open is the hook** (03–05): don't rush it. Land "pretty good, right?", voice the
  skeptic's doubt honestly, then let the vanilla trainwreck sit in silence before "you be the
  judge."
- **Meet Lumina** (02) is ambient: set it up in a sentence, keep talking; the scroll runs on
  its own and holds at the footer.
- **Callback:** when you hit the reflex render on 21 ("gradient, glass, neon"), point back:
  "same thing you watched happen in the first sixty seconds."
- **Silences that earn their keep:** the vanilla reveal (04), the 2022→2026 slop turn
  (11→12), and the Dusk money shot (20). Don't fill them.
- **Play-once videos** (03, 04, 27 shader): they hold on the final frame, keep talking; don't
  stop to watch. **Auto flurry** (29 tweets→meme): talk *over* it.
- **The one you build** (15, make-it-pop): click each meme on your beat; the escalation is
  the joke. Don't rush the clicks.
- **Relatable > clever.** The lines that land are the honest, familiar ones: the vanilla
  result speaking for itself, "Opus to center a div," the puzzle piece that fits nothing.
- **Credit Matt Pocock (leitwort) and radiant (the shader) out loud.**
- **Optional spoken jab** (no slide): the "You're absolutely right!" sycophancy tic; drop it
  wherever the model's over-eagerness comes up. Everyone's seen it.

## Q&A prep
- **"Isn't an adjective just a prompt?"** Covered on 21–22: yes, the *definition* is a
  prompt, a curated, shared one. The axis is ad-hoc vs. curated.
- **"Why no /auto / won't AI just do all this?"** Slide 30. One-shot doesn't work; design is
  iterative; ownership needs a human in the loop.
- **"Does it respect my design system?"** Yes: it reads PRODUCT.md / DESIGN.md first and
  steers inside your tokens, not around them.
- **"Will it work in my harness?"** It compiles to every major agent; install is one command.
