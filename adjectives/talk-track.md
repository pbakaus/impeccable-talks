## ACT I · Watch a word beat a sentence

**01 · A/B cold open** *(no words on screen — two results)*
- Open cold. Let them look for a second. "Same instruction — *make it bolder* — to two
  tools. On the left, a raw agent. On the right, Impeccable." Beat. "One of these you have
  seen a thousand times."
- 🎙️ "The gradient. The glassmorphism. The little ✨ *AI-powered* badge. Be honest — you
  can smell it. You've *shipped* it." (Let the room laugh at itself.)
- "By the end I want you to know exactly why the same word produced those two things."

**02 · Title**
- "I'm Paul. I build Impeccable. And I want to convince you that every design tool you own
  is aimed at the wrong altitude — and the fix is a word you already know."

## ACT II · The wrong altitude

**03 · The roles are blurring** *(designer / engineer → design engineer)*
- "For thirty years there were two people. A designer who decided, an engineer who built.
  Those lines are blurring — the designer codes, the coder designs, and the person who
  does both is who I'm here for."
- One line, keep moving: "Design isn't handed off anymore. It lives in the running product."

**04 · The spectrum** *(pixels · ? · prompts)*
- "Every design tool sits somewhere on this line. Two ends. Both are what we ship today.
  Both are wrong for a world where the designer *is* the engineer. The answer's the middle
  — and nobody built a tool there."

**05 · Too low. Pixels.** *(the inspector panel)*
- "The low end. Padding sliders, hex fields, tracking. One property at a time, by hand,
  silent about *why*. That's the altitude of handing pixels to an engineer —"
- 🎙️ "— except there's no engineer left to hand them to. It's just you, at 11pm, nudging
  a border-radius by one pixel, alone." (rueful; everyone's been there)

**06 · 2022 slop** *(full-bleed, no text)*
- "When I said slop a minute ago, this is what you pictured. Purple gradients,
  glassmorphism, six emoji. You called it on sight." Let it sit.

**07 · 2026 slop** *(full-bleed, the reveal)*
- "Here's the uncomfortable part." Beat. "*This* is also slop. It just put on a nice
  sweater. Cream, a tasteful serif, one accent — and six other startups have this exact
  page this week."
- Land it: "Slop is a moving target. You will never out-run it with a better prompt — the
  target moves faster than you can type."

**08 · You can't one-shot design** *(phrase)*
- "Which brings me to the fantasy a whole shelf of tools is selling: type a sentence, and
  the *problem of design* goes away. One shot."
- "It doesn't work. And on the rare day it *looks* like it worked, you own none of it, and
  the actual problem you had is still sitting there. You can generate a page in one shot.
  You cannot *design* in one shot."

**09 · Context-rich, multi-shot** *(~~one-shot~~)*
- "Real design is the opposite of one-shot. It's context-rich, and it's multi-shot. You
  give it a brief, and then you *iterate*. Both halves. Skip either one and you're back to
  slop-in-a-sweater."

## ACT III · The brief

**10 · The three questions** *(brief)*
- "The brief is where intent gets decided. And it's mostly three questions: what's the
  emotional territory, what should this *never* feel like, what's the reference." 
- 🎙️ "No AI design tool asks you a single one of these. They just… start. It's like hiring
  a designer who begins moving your logo around before you've said hello."

**11 · Nobody decided anything here** *(dissected slop, dimmed)*
- "Skip the brief and you still get *output*. You get this. Technically fine. And nobody
  decided anything. Mass-produce taste and you get *algorithmic Uniqlo* — perfectly
  pleasant, completely interchangeable."

## ACT IV · The answer: adjectives

**12 · The spectrum fills — adjectives** *(the answer)*
- "So back to the line. The altitude in the middle — the one nobody built for — is the one
  a creative director already works at. Adjectives."

**13 · The word wall** *(the vocabulary)*
- "Warmer. Leaner. Bolder. Distill. Add tension. This is the actual vocabulary — the
  controls a director thinks in. Not a parameter in sight."
- 🎙️ Honest aside: "Yes, half of these are verbs. *Distill*, *polish*. 'Adjective' is my
  shorthand for the altitude, not a grammar lecture — if a linguist's in the room, I'm
  sorry, and also please stay for the black hole."

**14 · Same brief, three adjectives** *(Dusk cards — the peak)*
- "Here's the proof it's a control and not a vibe. Same brief — a fragrance called Dusk.
  One default, what the model ships unasked. Then three decisions: bolder, quieter,
  distill. Same brief. Three points of view. *That's* an adjective doing work."

**15 · Bolder — how?** *(the objection + the reflex)*
- "Now the objection I'd raise if I were you: isn't an adjective just a short prompt? Watch
  what a raw model does with *make it bolder*." Gesture at the reflex render. "It reaches
  for the gradient, the glass, the neon. The *opposite* of bold. The word's wide open, so
  it fills the gap with its own defaults."

**16 · The definition**
- "What closes that gap is a *definition* both sides share. Impeccable's `/bolder` first
  throws out the reflexes — no gradients, no glass, no neon — and *then* says what bold
  actually is: hierarchy, scale, decisive type."
- The honest bit: "And yes — that definition is a prompt. You just shouldn't be *writing*
  it live. It's a prompt a community wrote, argued over, and hardened. The real axis was
  never pixels versus prompts. It's ad-hoc versus curated."

**17 · The test** *("if they believe you, you failed")*
- "My favorite test, straight from the skill: show someone your work and say 'AI made this
  bolder.' If they believe you — you failed."

**18 · An adjective is a leitwort** *(the coinage + credit)*
- "There's a name for a word like this. A *leitwort* — a leading word, from literary
  theory. Matt Pocock noticed it in AI skills: a word the agent repeats to steer its own
  behavior. That's exactly what a good adjective is. Give the model a leitwort, and it
  leads itself." *(Credit Matt out loud — he's one of us.)*

## ACT V · The honest caveat + what I learned

**19 · The descent** *(altitude shifts — this is the workflow)*
- "One honest caveat before you hold me to 'prompts are always wrong.' The right altitude
  *moves*. At the very start — deciding what the thing even is — a prompt is exactly right.
  At the very end, you want your hands on the pixels. Impeccable owns the productive middle."
- **Land the workflow point:** "And notice what that line actually is: it's a *workflow*.
  Explore, iterate, polish, ship. Impeccable's commands map to it on purpose."

**20 · Map the workflow first** *(the builder's lesson)*
- "This is the lesson I'd tattoo on every tool-builder in the room, including every skill
  author. You can build a beautiful tool — a perfect golden puzzle piece — and it gets zero
  adoption, because it doesn't fit the puzzle. The puzzle is the workflow."
- 🎙️ "I have built the gorgeous puzzle piece that fits nothing. More than once. It's a very
  expensive way to learn that nobody asked." → "Map the workflow first."

**21 · /overdrive** *(the event horizon shader — auto-playing)*
- Let it come up and just… let them look for a beat. "This is `/overdrive` — the command
  that pushes an interface past what's reasonable. Shaders, spring physics, 60fps."
- 🎙️ "I almost cut it. I thought it was too much — a little embarrassing, honestly. I ran
  it on radiant, expecting to hate it, and it built *this*." (gesture at the black hole)
  "It has a rabid fan base now. I ship what people pull, not what I'd defend on a slide."

**22 · An adjective with nothing behind it is just a nicer prompt** *(the lesson)*
- "The thing that *didn't* work: an adjective with nothing behind it. 'Warmer' with no
  brief just regresses to the average. The vocabulary rides on top of intent — it doesn't
  replace it. An adjective with nothing behind it is just a nicer prompt."

## ACT VI · The one it will never ship + close

**23 · /auto** *(tweets stamp in → the meme slams over — auto-playing)*
- Let the tweets pile up. Read a couple aloud, deadpan: "'when is /auto.' 'just add an auto
  mode and take my money.' 'can it just do it all for me, one button.'"
- When Michael Scott lands: 🎙️ just gesture at the screen and wait for the laugh. Then:
  "This is the single most-requested feature. And the answer is —" *(let the meme finish)*

**24 · There is no /auto** *(the answer)*
- "There is no `/auto`, and there never will be. It's the one-shot fantasy in a nicer coat.
  Hand design to `/auto` and you're right back to output without intent — you own none of
  it. Keeping a human in the loop isn't friction. It's the entire reason this works."

**25 · Taste can be amplified. It can't be lab-grown.**
- "Because here's what a year of this taught me: you cannot teach a model taste. We tried —
  it doesn't move. What you *can* do is hand it sharper instruments and leave the seeing to
  the person holding them. Taste can be amplified. It can't be lab-grown."

**26 · Close** *(title reprise + CTA)*
- "So: not pixels, not prompts. The word your expert already thinks in — for design, the
  adjective. It's open source, it runs in your agent today, and I'd love for you to break
  it. Design at the speed of adjectives. Thank you."
- CTA on screen: impeccable.style · open source · @pbakaus.

## Q&A prep
- **"Isn't an adjective just a prompt?"** Covered on 15–16: yes, the *definition* is a
  prompt — a curated, shared one. The axis is ad-hoc vs. curated.
- **"Why no /auto / won't AI just do all of this?"** Slide 24. One-shot doesn't work;
  design is iterative; ownership requires a human in the loop.
- **"Does it respect my design system?"** Yes — it reads PRODUCT.md / DESIGN.md first and
  steers inside your tokens, not around them.
- **"Will it work in my harness?"** It compiles to every major agent; install is one
  command.
